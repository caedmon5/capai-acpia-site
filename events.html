Datacolonialism vs. Data-Company-town: why the metaphor matters

Speaker: Frank Onuh, Humanities Innovation Lab
Date: Thursday, April 10, 2026
Time: 3:00 PM
Location: Digital Humanities Lab, B838 (in-person only)

Abstract

This talk examines the roots of the "datacolonialism" metaphor and argues that it misunderstands both colonialism and how exploitative data practices work in the platform economy. Instead it proposes the idea of the "Data Company Town": a form of rentier capitalism that trades convenience for exploitation.

About the Speaker

Frank Onuh is a PhD researcher at the University of Lethbridge, affiliated with the Humanities Innovation Lab. His doctoral research centres on decolonizing information verification practices in Africa, developing intelligent systems for automated fact-checking that capture the African experience. He is the founder and Executive Director of the AIRI Foundation, focused on AI literacy, digital empowerment, and misinformation studies, and a DRI EDIA Champion funded by the Digital Research Alliance of Canada. His published work includes "Scarlet Cloak and the Forest Adventure: A Preliminary Study of the Impact of AI on Commonly Used Writing Tools" (Springer, International Journal of Educational Technology in Higher Education, 2025), co-authored with colleagues at the Humanities Innovation Lab.

Eliza's Ghosts: Do Mental Health Chatbots Work and For Whom?

Speaker: Catherine Stinson, Queen's University
Date: Friday, April 17, 2026
Time: 3:00 - 4:30 PM Eastern
Location: Virtual (Zoom) (online)

Abstract

Millions of people are turning to chatbots for mental health support. These tools promise accessibility, affordability, and scalability — but raise serious concerns about safety, effectiveness, and algorithmic bias affecting marginalized communities who need mental healthcare access most.

About the Speaker

Catherine Stinson is an Assistant Professor in the School of Computing & Philosophy Department at Queen's University.
